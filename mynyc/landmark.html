Places you may wish to visit include:


The Staten Island Railway

  The Staten Island Railway, also known as the Staten Island Railroad and the Staten Island Rapid Transit, is the only train in Staten Island, running mostly parallel to Hylan Boulevard from St.George with the Staten Island Ferry in the north to Tottenville in the south. It is completley free until Clifton, upon which a metrocard is required in order to stop people from avoiding the St.George Terminal fee. More info can be seen on it's Wikipedia Page or the Mta Website.

The Staten Island railroad at Jefferson Avenue Station

Alice Austen House

  The Alice Austen House, located at 2 Hylan Blvd, is a historical site that used to be the home of victorian photographer Alice Austen (1866 – 1952). As stated on the Alice Austen Museum Website, "The House and its surrounding waterfront park are a nationally designated site of LGBTQ+ history, centering on the 56 year relationship between Alice Austen and her life partner Gertrude Tate, providing an important window into pre-Stonewall LGBTQ+ history and enriching our understanding of the important life and work of Alice Austen". To find out more, you can visit AliceAusten.org.

Last Chance Pond Park

  The Last Chance Pond Park is a park just to the left of Hylan Boulevard, named after the the Last Chance Pond and Wilderness Foundation who helped preserve the park in the 1960s. It consists of 3.90 acres of undeveloped wetlands, home to a diverse collection of protected plantlife. It was administered as a protected park to the Department of Citywide Administrative Services on December 30, 1999. To find out more, you can visit their NYC government park's page or it's Wikipedia page.

The Boulevard

  Completed in 2020, The Boulevard is a new Shopping center, in New Dorp. It that consists of a variety of shops and restraunts, including a ShopRite, an Alamo Drafthouse Cinema, and a LA Fitness. To view their full directory, you can head to their official website.

Great Kills Park

  Great Kills Park, introduced in 1947 by Robert Moses, was operated by the NYC Department of Parks and Recreation until 1972 where it was then incorporated into the National Park Service when Gateway National Recreation was established. It consists of four beaches, being the New Dorp, Cedar Grove, Oakwood, and Fox beaches. It also consists of a variety of recreational facilities, inclulding a beach center, a life-guarded beach, a multi-use path, hiking and biking trails, fishing areas, a marina and a boat launch. More information such as history, directions, and permit info can be found on the Wikipedia page, the NYC government parks website or the National Park Service government website

Mount Loretto Unique Area

  Mount Loretto Unique Area is a 241 acre open space and nature reserve protected by the NY state Department of Environmental Conservation in the south shore of Staten Island. Previously the home to the largest orphanage in NY state starting in 1890 by Father John Christopher Drumgoole, the orphanage was closed in 1994 and was replaced by two public schools, a senior and a community center, a food pantry and a day care facility. The Orphanage also had a past of sexual and physical abuse, brought foward by the 2019 Childrens Victim Act, as detailed in this Staten Island Live news article. Other info such as the environmental area and it's geographical history can be found on it's NY state Department of Environmental Conservation Website or it's Wikipedia page

The Conference House

  The Conference House, also known as the Billopp House after its owner Captain Christopher Billopp, is located at 7455 Hylan Blvd, the very south of the Staten Island. It is also the location of the Staten Island Peace Conference of 1766, a failed attempt at a peace treaty between the colonies and Great Britain during the Revolutionaty War, just less than 3 months after the formal Declaration of Independence. To learn more, you can visit their website, TheConferenceHouse.org.
